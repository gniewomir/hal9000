Alexander Kruel, ffs, you are mixing two separate topics again: the usefulness of "AI" and "intelligence."

The first is guaranteed, as H100s and H200s do not have to sleep, eat, shit, or socialize. If you run enough of them to power an LLM in a loop over a problem requiring pattern recognition—in a domain where they were trained and guard-railed by the syntax of programming languages—there will be impressive results. 

They are impressive because a human would kill themselves three times over from sheer boredom trying to do it by hand. "AI" in its current understanding, can be both a "stochastic parrot" and an incredibly useful, game-changing tool, because its mimicry gets better with each generation of LLMs. It is so good, in fact, that pretty smart people mistake it for intelligence.

But this does not support the claim that there is "intelligence" behind it. For now, they are just—better and better with each passing day—a mathematical representation of *our* world model expressed as language. And thats why, when stripped from this crutch they fail miserably [ARC-AGI-3: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24621]. Which, once again - does not reflect on the great utility they provide.

Understanding this distinction is probably most important bit of knowing when and how most efficiently employ LLMs, but stands in the way of starting another shitstorm on the internet. :)
